### Element-by-element specs

#### 01 · Gold Quote Divider

A Caribbean proverb in BABE voice (from `MHB-REMIXED-PROVERBS-COLLECTION-1774612839538.md`).

- Acts as palate cleanser between sections
- Sets the tone for the next pattern
- Visual: Cormorant italic, gold, centred
- Length: 1-2 lines max

#### 02 · Pattern Activation

The opening of the section. Milton Model pacing → leading.

- Format: Soft questions or gentle observations she'll likely recognise
- "You have probably noticed..." / "There's a particular way you..."
- Length: 2-4 sentences
- Goal: Match her experience first, then introduce the pattern name

Example template: "You have probably noticed that [common experience related to pattern]. Most women would call this [common framing]. What's actually moving here is [the pattern name]."

#### 03 · Receipts

The multi-lens evidence. This is what differentiates The BABE Signature from generic astrology readings.

- Header: "THE RECEIPTS" in gold uppercase
- Bulleted list, 3-7 items
- Each receipt: lens name + specific finding
- Lens names in gold/weight 500
- Format examples:
  - "Tropical Astrology: Sun in Libra 6th house"
  - "Destiny Cards: King of Hearts Birth Card"
  - "Numerology: Life Path 9 + Personal Year 7"
- For products at flagship tier, target 5-7 receipts per pattern
- For mid-tier, 3-5 receipts per pattern
- For Engine 1 passive (Life Spread), simplified to 2-3 lens confirmations

#### 04 · Locked In

Identity statement of the resourced version of the pattern.

- Format: "You are the woman who..." or "You move through the world as..."
- Tone: affirming, grounded, specific
- Length: 4-6 sentences
- NEVER vague generalities. Name actual behaviours, not feelings.

Visual: emerald-bordered box with "LOCKED IN" eyebrow.

#### 05 · Checked Out

The shadow expression of the same pattern. Externalised, never personalised.

- Format: "The pattern of [behaviour] is what shows up when..."
- Tone: compassionate, descriptive, never shaming
- Length: 4-6 sentences
- Frame: "This pattern was protection. It worked. Now you get to choose."
- NEVER "your problem of..." → ALWAYS "the pattern of..."

Visual: magenta-bordered box with "CHECKED OUT" eyebrow.

#### 06 · Body Signal

Chakra translation. Where the pattern lives in her body when active.

- Format: "When this pattern is running [Locked In or Checked Out], you might notice..."
- Specifics: physical sensations, breath patterns, posture changes
- Length: 2-4 sentences
- Cross-reference: Lens 7 (Chakras) data drives this

#### 07 · Old Script

The limiting belief in her voice. First-person.

- Format: "I used to think... / I keep telling myself..."
- Tone: honest, recognisable, not performative
- Length: 2-3 sentences
- Should sound like something she's actually said in her head

#### 08 · New Script

The identity reframe. Same first-person, expanded.

- Format: "What I'm starting to see is... / The truer version of this is..."
- Tone: liberating, not corrective
- Length: 2-3 sentences
- Frame: expansion of identity, not correction of behaviour

#### 09 · Pattern Interrupt

Format shift mid-section. Snaps attention.

- Could be: a single bold question, a paradox, a direct address, a small visual element
- Examples:
  - "When was the last time someone asked you what you actually wanted?"
  - "What if this isn't broken? What if it's protection that worked?"
  - "Read this slowly: [single line in larger gold italic]"
- Length: 1-2 lines

#### 10 · Three Moves

Practical applications, three scales of action.

- **1-Minute Move:** something she can do right now (breath, gesture, single sentence)
- **Conversation Move:** a script or framing for an actual conversation
- **Environment Move:** a structural shift in her space, schedule, or relationships

Each move is 2-3 sentences. Specific, not generic. Frame as options, never instructions.

Format: "You might try..." / "Some women find that..." / NEVER "You should..."

#### 11 · Future Pacing

Sensory-rich visualisation. First person, present tense, full VAKOG (visual, auditory, kinaesthetic, olfactory, gustatory).

- Length: 4-6 sentences
- Tense: present
- Voice: "I notice that I... / I feel my shoulders... / I hear myself saying..."
- Goal: rehearse the resourced state in her nervous system

Example: "I notice that when this pattern is online cleanly, I move through my mornings with a different rhythm. The kettle is on. There's coffee. I take three breaths before I check my phone. My shoulders are softer. I hear myself say no to the thing that doesn't fit, and the no is matter-of-fact, not apologetic."

#### 12 · Decision Filter

Single question she can run decisions through whenever this pattern is active.

- Format: One sentence question
- Specific to the pattern, not generic
- Length: 1 line, gold callout style

Examples:
- "Is this me leading from full, or me leading from depleted?"
- "Did she ask for advice, or is she asking to be heard?"
- "Am I saying yes from my body, or from old guilt?"

#### 13 · Results Markers

Three concrete signs the pattern is integrating cleanly. Body / Behaviour / Relational.

- **Body:** specific physical evidence (e.g., "shoulders drop without you noticing")
- **Behaviour:** specific action change (e.g., "you say no without explanation")
- **Relational:** specific shift in others' responses (e.g., "people stop asking you to fix things")

Each marker is 1-2 sentences. Concrete, observable.

#### 14 · Compass Support

Medicine Wheel direction guidance. Lens 8 data drives this.

- Names which compass direction supports this pattern's healing
- Brief description of what that direction is asking for
- Format: "The [direction] of your wheel is asking..."
- Length: 3-4 sentences

#### 15 · Integration Prompt

Reflection question + journaling prompt. Meta-Model precision question.

- Header: "INTEGRATION PROMPT" or "SIT WITH THIS"
- Single deep question that opens the pattern further
- Format: question, then short reflection prompt for journal
- Length: 2-3 sentences total

Example: "When did you first learn that being needed was the only way to be loved? Take 10 minutes today. Write whatever surfaces. Don't edit."

#### 16 · Compliance Footer

End of section grounding line.

- Format: italicised single line in grey
- "Pattern recognition, not prediction. The lived experience is the authority."
- Variant for shadow-heavy sections: "If your lived experience disagrees with anything in this section, trust your lived experience."

## NLP 8-STEP SEQUENCE (EMBEDDED, NEVER LABELLED)

The language architecture inside every full pattern section. Embedded inside the 16 elements above. Never named to the client.

### 1. PACING (Milton Model)
"You have probably noticed..." — match her experience before introducing anything new.
**Lives in:** Element 02 (Pattern Activation)

### 2. VALIDATION (Solution-focused)
Name the strength before the challenge. 3:1 affirming-to-challenging ratio.
**Lives in:** Element 04 (Locked In) before Element 05 (Checked Out)

### 3. REFRAMING
Old Script → New Script. Same trait, new context. Weakness becomes strength when read right.
**Lives in:** Elements 07 (Old Script) and 08 (New Script)

### 4. PATTERN INTERRUPT
Unexpected question, paradox, format shift. Snaps attention.
**Lives in:** Element 09 (Pattern Interrupt)

### 5. FUTURE PACING
Sensory-rich visualisation. First person, present tense, full VAKOG.
**Lives in:** Element 11 (Future Pacing)

### 6. ANCHORING
Connect resourced state to a reproducible cue (gesture, breath, phrase).
**Lives in:** Element 10 (Three Moves) — specifically the 1-Minute Move

### 7. IMPLEMENTATION INTENTION
"When I notice [signal], I [action]." Mandatory in mid-tier and flagship products.
**Lives in:** Element 12 (Decision Filter) — in if-then format

### 8. EMBEDDED COMMANDS
Marked formatting within natural sentences.
**Lives in:** distributed throughout sections, especially Element 11 (Future Pacing)

Example: *"As you read this section, you may begin to notice..."*

## IDENTITY FRAMING (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

Every report uses identity framing, never behavioural prescription.

### Identity framing examples
- "You are the woman who..." (NOT "you should...")
- "You move through the world as..." (NOT "you need to start...")
- "The truer version of this is..." (NOT "fix this by...")

### Why this rule exists
Behavioural prescription requires the client to change. Identity framing reveals what was always there. The pattern shifts when she recognises herself, not when she's told what to do.

This is also the compliance backbone. Identity reflection is not therapy. Behavioural prescription would be.

### Permitted action language
The Three Moves element (Element 10) is the ONLY place where action language is appropriate. Even there, frame as options:

- "You might try..."
- "Some women find that..."
- "When this pattern is online, you can..."

NEVER:
- "You should..."
- "You need to..."
- "Your task is..."
- "Do this..."
