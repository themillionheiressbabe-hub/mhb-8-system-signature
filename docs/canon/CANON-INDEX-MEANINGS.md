## ATTRACTION INDEX (-4 to +9)

The pull. The magnetism. How drawn the two are to each other regardless of compatibility.

### -4 to -1 · Attraction Aversion

Something in the dynamic actively repels. Doesn't mean conflict — sometimes it means quiet avoidance, friction without heat, or the kind of bond where you can be in the same room and feel nothing.

**Locked In:** Healthy distance. Not every connection is meant to be magnetic. Aversion can be wisdom — your body's way of saying "this isn't for me." When held with awareness, an aversion connection is one where both people respect the lack of pull and don't try to manufacture closeness.

**Checked Out:** When aversion gets ignored, both people perform connection that isn't real. They might force closeness for family obligation, business necessity, or social pressure. Resentment builds. Time spent together feels longer than it is.

### 0 · Neutral

No pull either way. The two might respect each other, work well together, or be related, but there's no magnetic charge.

**Locked In:** Functional respect without intensity. Often the healthiest dynamic for business partnerships or extended family relationships.

**Checked Out:** Mistaking neutrality for compatibility. Sometimes people stay in neutral relationships expecting the magnetism to develop. It usually doesn't.

### +1 to +3 · Mild Attraction

Light pull. They enjoy each other's company. Not magnetic, but warm.

**Locked In:** Easy companionship. The kind of bond that makes good friendships, comfortable family ties, sustainable partnerships. No drama, no obsession, just real warmth.

**Checked Out:** Mistaking mild for romantic. Sometimes mild attraction in a friendship gets confused for "something more" and pushes the connection past what it can hold.

### +4 to +6 · Strong Attraction

Real magnetism. They want to be near each other. Not obsessive, but pulled.

**Locked In:** This is where many of the most enduring romantic and creative partnerships live. Strong enough to want to build something. Stable enough to not consume each other.

**Checked Out:** Strong attraction without depth becomes infatuation. They might keep choosing each other based on the pull alone, missing that the underlying values don't match.

### +7 to +9 · Magnetic / Compelling

The kind of pull that's hard to walk away from. Often described as fated, undeniable, or "I knew the moment I met them."

**Locked In:** When this is held with consciousness, it can fuel some of the most generative bonds in a person's life. Soul-recognition, twin-flame energy, the partnership that catalyses both people's most important work.

**Checked Out:** Magnetic attraction without other anchors (compatibility, shared values, mutual respect) can become obsessive, codependent, or destructive. The pull alone is not enough to build on. The work is checking that the magnetism is matched by mutual care, not just intensity.

## INTENSITY INDEX (0 to +9)

The weight. How much this connection asks of you. How deep it cuts.

### 0 to +1 · Light

Surface-level connection. Pleasant but not consuming.

**Locked In:** Casual relationships, acquaintances, professional bonds. The lightness is a feature — you can enjoy it without it taking over your inner life.

**Checked Out:** Light connections kept past their time. Sometimes people stay in low-intensity relationships out of habit, missing that they've grown past them.

### +2 to +4 · Moderate

Real connection but bounded. The relationship matters but doesn't consume.

**Locked In:** Many of the most sustainable bonds live here. Close friendships, healthy partnerships, family relationships that have found their rhythm. Real but not all-consuming.

**Checked Out:** Moderate intensity sometimes gets confused for "not enough." People might leave moderately intense bonds expecting deeper connections elsewhere, only to realise they had real depth without recognising it.

### +5 to +6 · Significant

The connection takes up real space in your life. You think about them. Their decisions affect yours.

**Locked In:** Long-term partnerships, deep friendships, primary family ties. The intensity is matched by structure (commitment, shared building, real respect). When this works, it's the bond that shapes your life.

**Checked Out:** Significant intensity without structure becomes preoccupation. You might find yourself ruminating, over-investing, or losing your own life to the bond.

### +7 to +9 · Profound / Consuming

The kind of connection that changes you fundamentally. Soul-level. Often described as life-altering.

**Locked In:** When matched with high compatibility and conscious effort, profound intensity creates the bonds people remember at the end of their lives. The relationships that shaped who they became.

**Checked Out:** Profound intensity in low-compatibility pairs is the recipe for the relationships that look like fate but feel like a slow erosion. The depth doesn't equal the rightness. Sometimes the deepest pull is to a teaching, not a partnership.

## COMPATIBILITY INDEX (-9 to +9)

The structural ease or friction. How easily two Life Spreads align.

### -9 to -7 · Severe Friction

Almost everything is hard. Different rhythms, different values, different operating systems.

**Locked In:** Rare to find Locked In here. When it appears, it's usually short-term: two people who came together to teach each other something specific. Both grow because the friction forces both to articulate what they actually want.

**Checked Out:** Severe friction sustained becomes attrition. Both people become smaller versions of themselves trying to adapt. Most relationships at this level dissolve, and that dissolution is often a relief for both. The work is recognising when the lesson has landed.

### -6 to -4 · Significant Friction

Real differences across multiple domains. Communication takes effort. Shared decisions take negotiation.

**Locked In:** Some of the most generative bonds live here when both people are committed to the work. The friction surfaces what would otherwise stay buried. Both grow because they can't coast.

**Checked Out:** Significant friction without commitment becomes constant low-level conflict. Both people become defensive, rigid, or resentful. The energy that could have built something gets spent on translation.

### -3 to -1 · Mild Friction

Differences exist but are manageable. Most decisions land. Most conversations work.

**Locked In:** Many sustainable relationships live here. Real differences keep the bond honest. Real commonalities keep it functional.

**Checked Out:** Mild friction ignored becomes resentment that surfaces years later. The work is naming the small mismatches as they appear, not waiting until they pile up.

### 0 · Neutral

No structural ease, no structural friction. Functional but not flowing.

**Locked In:** Often appears in business partnerships or extended family. The neutrality is a feature — neither person has expectations of deep alignment.

**Checked Out:** Mistaking neutral for compatible. Sometimes people choose long-term partnerships based on neutral compatibility expecting it to deepen. It rarely does.

### +1 to +3 · Mild Compatibility

Things flow more often than they don't. Decisions land. Communication works.

**Locked In:** Sustainable bonds. Comfortable companionship. The kind of compatibility that makes daily life smooth.

**Checked Out:** Mild compatibility lulled into complacency. The ease becomes a reason to stop showing up. Both people coast.

### +4 to +6 · Strong Compatibility

Real structural alignment. Shared rhythms, shared values, shared operating systems on multiple domains.

**Locked In:** Many of the most enduring partnerships live here. Strong enough to make decisions easily. Aligned enough to build something together.

**Checked Out:** Strong compatibility can mask real issues. Because the daily fit is so easy, deeper differences (around money, sex, ambition, values) might not surface until much later. The work is checking the surface ease against the deep alignment.

### +7 to +9 · Profound Compatibility

The kind of alignment that feels like coming home. Multiple layers of structural fit.

**Locked In:** Soul partners, lifetime collaborators, the family bonds that hold across decades. When matched with conscious effort, this creates the bonds people describe as "we just fit."

**Checked Out:** Profound compatibility without effort becomes complacency at scale. Both people stop growing because the bond is so easy. The work is bringing intentional growth to a relationship that doesn't demand it.

## INDEX COMBINATIONS

The three indexes don't read in isolation. The combination tells the real story.

### High Attraction + High Compatibility + High Intensity
The classic "soul partner" reading. Magnetic pull, structural fit, deep weight. When held with conscious care, these become defining bonds.

### High Attraction + Low Compatibility + High Intensity
The intense bond that doesn't actually fit. Magnetic and weighty but structurally misaligned. These are often the relationships that look like fate but feel like erosion. Critical to read carefully — these dynamics often need the most explicit naming.

### Low Attraction + High Compatibility + Low Intensity
Functional alignment without magnetism. Often appears in family or business partnerships. Healthy when both people accept the bond for what it is.

### High Attraction + High Compatibility + Low Intensity
The easy, fun, well-fitted bond that doesn't ask much. Often friendships, casual partnerships, comfortable family ties.

### Low Attraction + Low Compatibility + Low Intensity
Connections that don't need to be active. Acquaintances, distant family, former colleagues. Healthy when allowed to be exactly what they are.

### Saturn-heavy connections regardless of indexes
When 2+ Saturn codes appear, the indexes need extra context. Saturn binding can amplify intensity without affecting attraction. Compatibility index alone won't capture the dynamic. The Saturn safety footer attaches automatically — but the report itself needs to read the Saturn weight on top of the indexes.

## VISUAL DISPLAY (BOND REPORT)

The 3 indexes appear at the top of every Bond report as a visual bar.

### Visual spec

**Container:**
- Full-width section
- Background: navy lighter
- Border: 1px gold at 25% opacity
- Border radius: 16px
- Padding: 32px

**Each index bar:**
- Label: index name in Cormorant italic, 18px
- Range: shown as scale (e.g., "-4 to +9")
- Bar: horizontal, 12px tall, gradient from danger zone (left) through neutral (centre) to high zone (right)
- Marker: filled circle at the index value position, suit-coloured to Person A's Birth Card
- Value display: number to the right of the bar in Outfit weight 600
- One-line interpretation below: in Outfit 14px, grey-mid

**Example render:**
```
ATTRACTION INDEX                      ●─── +6
-4 ────── 0 ────── +9                 Strong magnetism

INTENSITY INDEX                  ●──────── +4
0 ─────────────── +9                  Moderate weight

COMPATIBILITY INDEX                ●──── +5
-9 ────── 0 ────── +9                 Strong structural alignment
```

### Why visual matters
The bars give the client an at-a-glance sense of the dynamic before they read the depth. Numbers alone feel clinical. The visual makes the indexes feel readable.

## INTEGRATION WITH REPORT GENERATION

When the Bond Builder generates a draft:

1. The 3 index values are passed to Claude API as part of the prompt
2. Claude pulls the matching range interpretations from this canon
3. The generated report integrates index meanings into prose (NOT as numerical readouts)
4. Index values still appear at the top of the report as visual bars
5. The Locked In/Checked Out framing of each index range threads into the relevant sections

### Example integration

If Attraction = +7:
- Top of report: visual bar showing +7 with "Magnetic / Compelling" label
- Throughout the report: prose references the magnetic quality without naming the number ("There's a pull between you that doesn't soften with time...")
- Compatibility section: reads the magnetism in context of the structural fit
- Closing: integration prompts that honour the depth of the pull without romanticising it

The numbers stay backstage. The meanings lead.

## PUBLIC LANGUAGE RULES

### What clients see
- The visual index bars (with labels like "Magnetic / Compelling")
- Prose interpretations woven through the report
- The number itself (e.g., "+7") on the bar marker

### What clients DON'T see
- The Cardology Toolkit source language
- The numerical range tables
- "MATCH index" or other technical terminology
- The cross-comparison logic between the three indexes

### Why this matters
The numbers are useful as anchors but become reductive if treated as scores. A "+7 attraction" isn't a grade. It's a quality of pull. The report's job is to translate the quality, not display the score.
