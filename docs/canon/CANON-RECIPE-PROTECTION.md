# CANON-RECIPE-PROTECTION
## The MillionHeiress BABE™ | Locked Share vs Protect Boundaries
## Version: 1.0 | April 2026

---

## 1. THE CORE RULE

**Show the receipts. Never show the recipe.**

The receipts prove the work is real. The recipe is what makes it ours.

We share what patterns look like in real life. We never share how we cross-check, weight, or assemble them.

---

## 2. SHARE FREELY (PUBLIC-FACING)

These can live anywhere. TikTok, blog, sales pages, emails, podcasts.

### Pattern outcomes
What patterns look like when they show up in someone's life. Real behaviours, real situations, real receipts.

✅ "This pattern shows up as saying yes to projects three weeks before you have the capacity for them."

### Old script vs new script reframes
The before/after of how someone moves when locked in versus checked out.

✅ "Old script: I have to earn rest. New script: rest is what makes the work possible."

### The existence of a multi-system approach
That there's more than one lens. That cross-verification matters. That patterns confirmed across systems are stronger than patterns from one source.

✅ "We don't use one system. We use multiple systems and only name patterns when several agree."

### Scale and depth of reports
Page counts, section counts, time investment, what's included.

✅ "The BABE Signature is 42-50 pages, 22 sections, takes 7-10 days to build."

### Yemi's personal examples
Stories from Yemi's own data, her patterns, what she's worked through. Her receipts are her own to share.

✅ "I'm a King of Hearts. That means I lead with my heart and that's both my power and my pressure point."

### Compliance language
Disclaimers, the client authority rule, the entertainment positioning.

### Voice and methodology principles
That the work is non-predictive, identity-framed, shadow-inclusive, body-aware.

## 3. PROTECT ALWAYS (INTERNAL ONLY)

These never appear in public-facing content. Anywhere. Ever.

### The number of lenses
We never say "seven" publicly. We say "multiple systems," "several lenses," "a layered approach."

❌ "Our seven-lens system..."
✅ "Our multi-system approach..."

### The names of the lenses
We never list which systems we cross-check.

❌ "We use Tropical Astrology, Sidereal Astrology, Destiny Cards, Numerology, Chinese Zodiac, Chakras, and Medicine Wheel."
✅ "We use a layered approach across multiple recognised pattern frameworks."

People can guess. They can't have it confirmed.

### The cross-verification rule
The rule that nothing enters a report unless 3+ systems confirm it. This is the engine. It stays internal.

❌ "We only include patterns confirmed by at least three systems."
✅ "We only include patterns we can verify across multiple sources."

### How systems are weighted
That Tropical Astrology is the primary anchor. That Sidereal is cross-reference only. The order of priority. The decision rules.

### How reports are assembled
The 16-element section format. The NLP sequence (Pacing → Validation → Reframing → Pattern Interrupt → Future Pacing → Anchoring → Implementation Intention → Embedded Commands). The 3:1 affirming-to-challenging ratio. The shadow proportions.

### The verification rules
What counts as a confirmed pattern. What gets rejected. The internal QC process.

### The internal engine structure
The Pattern Engine. The Birthprint composer. The cross-verification logic. Database structure. API workflow.

### Client data
Other clients' birthprints, patterns, names, situations. Even anonymised. Even with permission. The default is silence.

### Proprietary NLP methods
The specific NLP frameworks used in section construction. The embedded commands. The Milton Model and Meta Model integration.

### The prompts
The Claude prompts that draft sections. The system prompts. The content generators. None of these go public.

### House systems and technical specs
Placidus for Tropical. Whole Sign for Sidereal. Lahiri Ayanamsa. These are technical decisions, not marketing language.

## 4. THE GREY ZONE (HANDLE WITH CARE)

These can appear publicly in specific contexts but not casually.

### Specific lens names
Yemi can mention them in **her own** content (her personal examples, her own birthprint, her TikToks about her own patterns). She is the system. She can name what she's reading in her own life.

What we never do: list all of them as the public methodology.

### Pattern names
Internal pattern names ("the work-as-identity pattern," "the voice-and-worth pattern") can surface in content if they're useful. They don't need to be presented as the proprietary internal taxonomy.

### NLP language patterns
We use NLP. We don't market it as NLP unless the audience is sophisticated. With wider audiences: "language that lands" or "how the report reads."

### The Pattern Engine
We can reference "the system" or "the engine" generally. We never describe how it works.

## 6. THE TIKTOK TEST

Before any TikTok script goes out, run it past this:

1. **Does it name lenses?** If yes, rewrite unless it's a personal Yemi example.
2. **Does it explain the cross-checking method?** If yes, rewrite.
3. **Does it reveal report assembly logic?** If yes, rewrite.
4. **Could a competitor watch this and replicate the methodology?** If yes, rewrite.

If all four come back clean, it's safe.

## 9. THE COMPETITOR PROTECTION

Why this matters: anyone with a copy of the locked rules could attempt to replicate the methodology. The recipe is what makes MHB defensible.

Three layers of protection:

1. **Public layer:** The brand, voice, products, and outcomes. Anyone can see this.
2. **Methodology layer:** That MHB uses multiple systems with cross-verification. People can know this exists. They can't see the rules.
3. **Engine layer:** The actual code, prompts, weights, decision rules. Internal only.

The first two are marketing. The third is the moat.

## 10. WHEN TO BREAK THE RULE

There are no situations where the recipe gets shared publicly.

Not for press. Not for podcasts. Not for partnerships. Not for trade. Not for trust-building. Not for "transparency."

If a podcast wants to know how the system works, the answer is: "I'd love to walk you through the experience of receiving a report. The methodology itself stays internal because it's the work I'm building to license one day."

That answer is enough. Anyone who pushes past it is not someone to do business with.
